    Cardiology

    Cardiology is the branch of medicine that deals with the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases and conditions related to the heart and blood vessels (the cardiovascular system).

    Neurology

    Neurology is the branch of medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and management of disorders of the nervous system, which includes the brain, spinal cord, and nerves throughout the body.

    Hepatology

    Hepatology is a specialized branch of medicine that focuses on the study, diagnosis, and treatment of diseases related to the liver, gallbladder, bile ducts, and pancreas.

    Pediatrics

    Pediatrics is the branch of medicine that focuses on the health and medical care of infants, children, and adolescents, typically from birth up to 18 years of age.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    At WorldSalubrity, we understand that staying informed is an important part of staying healthy. That’s why we’ve put together this Frequently Asked Questions section to provide clear, reliable answers to the health questions we hear most often.

    What are the basic steps to maintain good health?

    Maintain a balanced diet, exercise regularly, get enough sleep, manage stress, avoid harmful substances (like tobacco), and attend regular medical check-ups.

    How often should I go for a medical check-up?

    Adults should have a full check-up at least once a year, or more frequently if they have a chronic condition or are at risk for certain diseases.

    What are the most important vaccines for adults?

    Tetanus, influenza (flu), hepatitis B, COVID-19, and HPV (depending on age). Your doctor can guide you based on your health history.

    When should I see a doctor for a headache or fever?

    If the headache is severe, persistent, or accompanied by nausea, vision issues, or confusion — or if a fever lasts more than 3 days or exceeds 39°C (102°F), seek medical attention.

    What are common signs of high blood pressure?

    High blood pressure often has no symptoms. That’s why it’s called the “silent killer.” Regular monitoring is important for early detection.

    How do I know if my mental health needs attention?

    Persistent sadness, anxiety, mood swings, loss of interest in daily activities, or trouble sleeping may signal a need for professional support.
